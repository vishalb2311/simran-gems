Understanding the Four C's

Carat Weight

The larger the stone, the rarer — and the higher the price per carat.

Carat is the unit of measurement used to weigh diamonds. One carat equals 200 milligrams. Large diamonds are created less frequently by nature, commanding substantially higher prices than smaller stones of equal quality.

However, two diamonds of equal carat weight may have very different values depending on their cut, clarity and colour. A perfectly cut, flawless diamond can outvalue a much larger stone with weaker colour.

2.00ct+ · 8.2mm+
Eye-catching statement stones — the most rare and prized
1.00ct · 6.5mm
Most favoured size for solitaire engagement rings
0.50ct · 5.2mm
Excellent for diamond pendants and stud earrings
0.30ct · 4.2mm
Widely used in fine jewellery and watch settings

Colour

The less colour in a diamond, the better its grade — and the more brilliance it releases.

Diamonds are graded alphabetically from D (completely colourless) to Z+ (light yellow or brown). Colourless diamonds allow the most light to pass through, creating brilliant fire and scintillation.

Fluorescence — a reaction to UV light — is present in some diamonds but is not detectable under normal lighting. Diamonds with fluorescence at reduced prices can represent excellent value.

D · E · F
Colourless — the rarest and most valuable colour range
G · H · I · J
Near colourless — virtually undetectable, excellent value
K · L · M
Faint colour — visible when mounted, warm character
N – Z
Light to very light yellow — colour clearly perceptible

Clarity

Nature's fingerprint — every diamond's inclusions are entirely unique to that stone.

Diamonds may carry traces of the immense pressure of their formation, appearing as inclusions (internal) or blemishes (external). Clarity is graded under 10× magnification by trained gemologists.

We recommend choosing a diamond that is "eye-clean" — where inclusions are not visible to the naked eye. This typically means any grade from IF through to SI2.

FL · IF
Flawless / Internally Flawless — the pinnacle of clarity
VVS1 · VVS2
Tiny inclusions, extremely difficult to detect at 10×
VS1 · VS2
Minor inclusions, difficult to detect — excellent value
SI1 · SI2
Eye-clean inclusions detectable only at 10× — most popular

Cut

The only C entirely shaped by human craft — and the one that most determines brilliance.

Cut refers to a diamond's proportions, symmetry and polish. A well-cut diamond reflects light from one facet to another before dispersing it through the table as brilliance. In a poorly cut stone, light leaks from the sides and bottom.

Simran specialises in Triple Excellent, Very Good and Good make stones across our full range of certified and non-certified diamonds.
